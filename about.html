Why We Built Auditra

From paper chaos to proactive compliance.

I spent over a decade as a regional dietitian managing dietary compliance across multiple long-term care facilities. I knew the daily grind intimately—the shift huddles, the equipment logs, the deep-cleaning assignments, and the endless paper checklists.

I also knew the constant, underlying anxiety of missed documentation. Whether it was an enteral nutrition order change, a gap in the MAR/TAR, or a temperature log left blank, the risk of a survey deficiency was always looming. We were told that survey readiness is not something you prepare for, it's how you operate every day. But with paper binders and siloed information, operating proactively felt nearly impossible.

I realized that everything required to keep a facility survey-ready was either living on paper, buried in a binder, or trapped in one person's head. When staff turned over or a new building was acquired, that knowledge walked out the door.

That’s why I built Auditra. I wanted to take everything that used to live on paper and standardize it across an entire portfolio of facilities. Auditra isn't just a digital checklist; it's a centralized brain that identifies risks early, drives accountability, and ensures every building operates with the same standard of excellence—before the surveyor ever arrives.

Real-World Experience

Grounded in real regional dietitian experience.

For over 12 years, I lived and breathed long-term care dietary compliance. I spent my days walking through dozens of facility kitchens, conducting hands-on audits, and meticulously reviewing physician orders to ensure clinical accuracy.

I’ve stood in the trenches with nursing staff, training them on precise MAR/TAR documentation and enteral nutrition protocols. When state surveyors arrived, I was the one responding to findings, managing corrective actions, and trying to patch the gaps in our paper-based processes.

That time on the floor wasn’t just a job—it was the exact experience that shaped what Auditra checks for, how it categorizes risk, and why it’s built to keep facilities ready every single day, not just when a survey is looming.
